The Big 5 Close
Having a goal or a plan helps us stay motivated and energised. Goals give us something to look forward to and stop us dwelling on past problems.
  • Visualise your “ideal” life and then plan to do something simple that will help you to start to achieve your vision
  • Use an online calendar or notebook to help remind you of your goals and plans
  • Make a commitment each day to do something simple that will help improve or maintain your quality of life
Practical examples:
  • Clean or organise your home
  • Set simple goals for each week
  • Check-in on your goals regularly
  • Plan a day-trip or holiday
  • Write a list of your long-term goals

Routines we do automatically like our sleep routine, eating well or being active, are critical for good mental health. These actions set us up for the day.
  • Think about when you last felt grounded – what were your habits and routines?
  • Reflect on your work, home, relationship and ‘personal passion’ routines – what can you tweak?
  • Make small changes immediately, and remember to celebrate the improvements
Practical examples:
  • Set a regular sleep/wake time
  • Spend time outside each day
  • Drink lots of water
  • Set a simple, daily routine
  • Maintain work-time and home-time boundaries
  • Take regular breaks during your work

Regular contact with people we love and respect, which may include our family, friends or tribe, help us to feel validated and give us a sense of belonging.
  • Reflect on your connections; are you regularly connecting with the people you love and value?
  • Don’t wait for others to connect, pick up your phone and SMS a message to someone you miss
  • Remember that most people want to hear from you!
Practical examples:
  • Call/text someone you miss
  • Spend time with someone you care about
  • Watch a show with someone
  • Have coffee/meal with someone
  • Help a friend with something
  • Organise a group social activity

Good mental health doesn’t happen by chance—it’s built through daily habits. Research with over 20,000 people has revealed five type of actions that support mental well-being.


Developed by MindSpot, these actions are called "The Big 5" and doing them regularly is a great way to help you boost resilience, improve mood, and maintain a healthier mind.

❮
❯

What is it?

The Big 5 is a set of five proven actions that play a crucial role in maintaining mental well-being. Developed by MindSpot through research with over 20,000 individuals, The Big 5 consists of Meaningful Activities, Healthy Thinking, Goals and Plans, Healthy Routines, and Social Connections. These are not new or complex ideas—philosophers and scientists have discussed them for centuries—but research now shows that they form the foundation of good mental health. When practiced regularly, they help improve mood, build resilience, and create a sense of purpose. The best part? The Big 5 fits easily into daily life, requiring only small, consistent efforts to make a big impact.

How it works?

Research has identified a tipping point for mental health—people who engage in The Big 5 at least 3-4 times per week are much more likely to experience good mental health. On the other hand, those who neglect these activities are at a higher risk of anxiety, depression, and lower life satisfaction. While The Big 5 won’t eliminate life’s challenges, they provide the tools to handle stress, overcome setbacks, and maintain a healthier, more balanced mindset.

Key Messages

  • The Big 5 are essential for our mental health.
  • Not doing The Big 5 regularly is linked to poor mental health.
  • Doing The Big 5 regularly supports good mental health.
